# Code of Conduct

NestRS is a small, volunteer-run project, and the goal here is simple: keep it
a place where contributing — code, docs, questions, ideas — feels welcoming
and worth the effort. Everyone interacting in the repository, the
[Discussions](https://github.com/YV17labs/NestRS/discussions), the issue tracker,
and any other project space is expected to follow the rules below.

## What we expect

- **Be kind, even when you disagree.** Strong opinions are fine; condescension,
  sarcasm at someone's expense, and dismissive replies are not. Critique the
  idea, not the person.
- **Assume good intent, ask before assuming bad.** A confusing message is
  usually a language gap or a missing detail, not malice. Ask a clarifying
  question first.
- **Make room for newcomers.** Someone new to Rust, to NestRS, or to open
  source is not "wasting your time" by asking — they are the reason early
  contributors matter. Point them at the right doc or issue and move on.
- **Stay on topic and constructive.** Keep threads focused on the change at
  hand. Off-topic detours, recruiting, and self-promotion belong elsewhere.
- **Respect maintainer decisions.** A `wontfix` or a closed PR is not a
  personal slight — it is a scope call. Disagree once, explain why, then move
  on; reopening the same argument in five places is not persuasion.

## What is not acceptable

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments — including ones aimed at
  someone's identity, background, beliefs, or appearance.
- Harassment of any form: targeted hostility, unwanted contact, sustained
  pile-ons, or amplifying any of the above.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or advances.
- Publishing someone's private information (address, employer, real name when
  they use a handle, …) without their explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or deliberate bad-faith engagement.

These apply to every project space — public threads, private DMs about the
project, security advisories, conference talks, the whole surface.

## Reporting

If something crosses the line — toward you or toward someone else — tell the
maintainers. You do not have to be the target to report.

The fastest private channel is a **GitHub direct message to
[@yvanitou](https://github.com/yvanitou)**, or — if you prefer to keep it on
the repo — open a private advisory through the
[Security tab](https://github.com/YV17labs/NestRS/security) and mark it as a
Code of Conduct report; it stays confidential until a maintainer responds.

Include what happened, where, and (if helpful) links to the messages. Your
report stays confidential. Retaliating against a reporter is itself a
violation.

## What happens next

This is a small project, so there is no formal SLA — but you can expect:

1. An acknowledgement that the report was received.
2. A private review by the maintainers.
3. A decision and, if action is needed, a follow-up with the people involved.

Depending on what happened, that action can be a private note, an edited or
deleted message, a temporary cool-off, or a ban from the project's spaces.
Maintainers will explain the reasoning to the parties concerned and keep the
specifics out of public threads.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies wherever the project lives — the repository, its
discussions, issues, pull requests, advisories, and any space where someone is
clearly representing or interacting with NestRS.
